Qutub Minar

One of the most popular historical monuments of India and the tallest creations in Indian history is Qutub Minar. With its red sandstone storeys and beautiful Iranian architecture becomes one of the finest examples of Indo-Muslim structure. There are many things you can experience like an iron pillar and its uniqueness is that the pillar has not rusted even after thousands of years. Likewise, you can also see the first mosque here which is situated at the foot of the Qutub Minar.

Entry fee: For Foreigners: INR 250, For Indians: INR 10 Opening timing: From Monday to Sunday: 7 am to 5 pm
  

Taj Mahal

It is also known as “crown of places” which is built entirely with white marble. Taj Mahal is one of the 7 wonders of the world, so tourists from all over the world visit to know the importance of the historical places in India. And taj mahal is one of the most popular places to visit in India for travelers as well as for honeymoon couples. Besides, this masterpiece located in one of the golden triangle cities so you can also get a wonderful chance to explore the Agra attractions and streets.

Entry fee: For Foreigner: INR 1000, For Indian: INR 40 Opening timing: From Monday to Sunday: 6:30 am to 9:30 pm Remark: Closed on Friday
  

Ajanta and Ellora Caves

It is located in Aurangabad district of Maharashtra. Ajanta and Ellora Caves is also a part of the UNESCO World Heritage Site. This cave is one of the largest single monolithic rock-cut temples in the world and charming caves make it the epitome of beauty. As a fact, it is must-to-visit historical monuments of India for travelers. But also, the place offers you to explore the main 29 caves which shows the sculptures and paintings.

Entry fees: For Indians: INR 10, For Foreigners: INR 250
  

Victorial Memorial

This place is situated right in the heart of Kolkata and the structure is just elegant. Located right in the heart of Kolkata, this memorial has been named after Queen Victoria. The beautiful white marbled opulent structure is surrounded by lush green gardens which together cover 64 acres of land. You can explore small statues and sculptures to get the historical places information. Moreover, try to attend the evening show called ‘son et Lumiere’ on your visit. So, you can spend quality time with your friends or family by picking the place on your India tour packages.

Entry fee: For Indians: INR 20, For Foreigners: INR 200 Opening Timing:From Monday to Sunday: 10 am to 5 pm Remark: Closed on Mondays
  

Mysore Palace

This palace is located in the heart of the city Mysore in Karnataka state of India. This palace is built by a British architect in Indo Saracenic style which definitely captured your heart. Additionally, it is known as a museum that consists of royal pictures, clothing, and artifacts. So, you can experience the most of the place by visiting it during the Dasara festival. During this festival, the climate is the most pleasant and comfortable for travelers to explore the palace freely.

Entry fee: For Indians: INR 40, For Foreigners: INR 200 Opening Timing: From Monday to Friday: 10 am to 5:30 pm Remark: Closed on Sunday and Govt. Holidays
  

Lakshmi Vilas Palace

To sneak and peek more of Indo-Saracenic architecture, visit Lakshmi Vilas Palace. It is located in Vadodara city of Gujarat. This palace is the official residence of the maharaja of Vadodara and built by prestigious Gaekwad. Besides, you can get the royal vibes while exploring the palace. As it holds beautiful interiors and also one of the unknown historical places in India.

Entry fee: Palace:For Adults: INR 200 Museum: For Indians: INR 60, For Foreigners: INR 150 Opening Timing:From Monday to Sunday: 10 am to 5 pm
